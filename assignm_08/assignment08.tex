\section*{Exercise 3: Generating samples from a Gaussian distribution}
\subsection*{Task}

Show that in eigen decomposition, $A = S2 \cdot S2'$.

\subsection*{Solution}

We defined $V$ and $D$ by the eigendecomposition:

\[
  A = V \cdot D \cdot V^{-1}
\]

$A$ being symmetric and realvalued results in $V$ being orthogonal, thus $V^{-1} = V^t$.

Now $S2 = V \cdot \sqrt(D)$, therefore

\[
  S2 \cdot S2^t = V \cdot \sqrt(D) \cdot (V \cdot \sqrt(D))^t = V \cdot \sqrt(D)\cdot \sqrt(D)^t \cdot V^t = V \cdot D \cdot V^{-1} = A
\]
